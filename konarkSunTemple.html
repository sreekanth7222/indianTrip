Konark Sun Temple

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famed chariot-shaped temple dedicated to the Sun God, showcasing some of the finest stone carvings in India.

  • Location: Konark, Odisha
  • Era: 13th century CE (Eastern Ganga dynasty)
  • Also known as: Black Pagoda (historic sailors’ name)
  • Famous for: Stone chariot wheels, sculptures, and sun-aligned design

The Story & Significance

The Konark Sun Temple was built as a grand stone chariot for Surya, the Sun God, with twelve pairs of wheels and seven horses. Though partially in ruins today, it remains an outstanding example of Kalinga architecture.

Historic Timeline

  • 13th c.: Constructed under King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty.
  • Later centuries: Gradual decline and damage due to time and invasions.
  • 20th c.: Major conservation efforts by the Archaeological Survey of India.
  • Present day: Recognized globally as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Why Visitors Come

To admire the intricate wheels, erotic sculptures, and detailed stone work that tell stories of life, time, and divinity.

The annual Konark Dance Festival lights up the complex with classical performances under the open sky.
