Kashi Vishwanath Temple

The spiritual heart of Varanasi on the banks of the Ganga—one of the most revered Shiva Jyotirlingas, blending ancient lore with a vibrant living city.

  • Location: Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
  • Era: Current structure rebuilt in 1780 by Ahilyabai Holkar
  • Also known as: Shri Kashi Vishwanath Mandir, the Golden Temple
  • Famous for: Jyotirlinga darshan, Ganga Aarti, Kashi Corridor

The Story & Significance

Kashi is believed to be the city of Lord Shiva, eternal and indestructible. The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt multiple times, yet the devotion of pilgrims continues to shape its lanes, ghats, and rituals.

Historic Timeline

  • Ancient references: Featured in Puranas as the abode of Vishwanath.
  • 1669 CE: Medieval destruction; worship continued in nearby shrines.
  • 1780 CE: Reconstructed by Queen Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore.
  • 2021: Kashi Vishwanath Corridor inaugurated, opening space to the ghats.

Why Devotees Come

To perform darshan of the Jyotirlinga, take a holy dip in the Ganga, and offer Rudrabhishek puja within the garbhagriha.

Many combine the visit with sunrise boat rides, Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat, and a stroll through the newly built corridor.
