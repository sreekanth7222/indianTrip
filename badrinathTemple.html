Badrinath Temple

A vibrant mountain shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu, part of the Char Dham and Chota Char Dham, set along the banks of the Alaknanda river.

  • Location: Badrinath, Uttarakhand
  • Deity: Badri Narayan (Vishnu)
  • Also known as: Badrinarayan Temple
  • Famous for: Colorful façade, hot springs, and high-altitude darshan

The Story & Significance

Badrinath is said to be where Lord Vishnu meditated while Goddess Lakshmi took the form of a Badri tree to shelter him. The temple has long been a key stop for pilgrims seeking moksha in the Himalayas.

Historic Timeline

  • Ancient references: Featured in Vishnu-related Puranic legends.
  • 8th c. CE (tradition): Adi Shankaracharya is credited with re-establishing worship.
  • Later centuries: Expanded and renovated by Garhwal rulers.
  • Modern era: Improved access roads and seasonal facilities for yatris.

Why Devotees Come

To bathe in the Tapt Kund hot springs, receive darshan of Badri Narayan, and experience the unique combination of snow peaks and colorful temple architecture.

Many combine Badrinath with Kedarnath and other Char Dham shrines for a complete yatra.
