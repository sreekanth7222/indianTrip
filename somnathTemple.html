Somnath Temple

The first of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, standing on the shores of the Arabian Sea—rebuilt across ages as a symbol of faith and resilience.

  • Location: Prabhas Patan, Gir Somnath, Gujarat
  • Era: Referenced since antiquity; current temple rebuilt in 1951
  • Also known as: Deo Patan
  • Famous for: Jyotirlinga darshan, Triveni Sangam, evening aarti & light show

The Story & Significance

Somnath means “Lord of the Moon.” Legends say Soma (the Moon God) worshipped Shiva here to regain his radiance. The shrine has seen invasions and reconstructions, yet devotees rebuilt it each time, making it a living testament to devotion.

Historic Timeline

  • Ancient era: Mentioned in Skanda and Shiv Puranas as a prime Jyotirlinga.
  • 1025 CE: Mahmud of Ghazni’s raid; subsequent reconstructions by local kings.
  • 12th–18th c.: Rebuilt by Chaulukya rulers; later restored under Maratha patronage.
  • 1950–1951: Present structure initiated by Sardar Patel and K.M. Munshi.

Why Devotees Come

To seek blessings of the Somnath Jyotirlinga, bathe at the Triveni Sangam where Hiran, Kapila, and Saraswati meet the sea, and attend the serene evening aarti facing the horizon.

Visitors also enjoy the nightly sound-and-light show that recounts Somnath’s history and the courage of its patrons.
