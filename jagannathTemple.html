Jagannath Temple, Puri

One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, famous for Lord Jagannath and the grand Rath Yatra that brings the deities onto the streets.

  • Location: Puri, Odisha
  • Deities: Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra
  • Also known as: Srimandira
  • Famous for: Rath Yatra, Mahaprasad, and unique wooden idols

The Story & Significance

The temple is a major Vaishnava shrine and a vital stop in the Char Dham yatra. Its wooden idols are ceremonially replaced in a ritual called Nabakalebara, symbolizing renewal.

Historic Timeline

  • Ancient origins: Legends link the shrine to King Indradyumna.
  • 12th c.: Current stone temple structure completed by Ganga dynasty rulers.
  • Medieval period: Temple patronized by regional kings and Bhakti saints.
  • Today: Managed by Shree Jagannath Temple Administration (SJTA).

Why Devotees Come

To seek darshan of Lord Jagannath and relish Mahaprasad, cooked in special earthen pots stacked over fire.

The annual Rath Yatra, when the deities ride giant wooden chariots, draws millions to Puri’s Grand Road.
