Kedarnath Temple

A high-altitude Shiva temple nestled in the Himalayas, part of the Char Dham and Panch Kedar, where snow peaks surround timeless devotion.

  • Location: Kedarnath, Uttarakhand
  • Elevation: ~3,583 m (11,755 ft)
  • Also known as: Kedarnath Dham
  • Famous for: Himalayan trek, stone shrine, limited pilgrimage season

The Story & Significance

According to legends, the Pandavas sought Lord Shiva here for absolution after the Kurukshetra war. The current stone structure has withstood centuries of snow, rain, and even floods, symbolizing unshakable faith.

Historic Timeline

  • Ancient era: Linked to Mahabharata and Panch Kedar tradition.
  • 8th c. CE (tradition): Adi Shankaracharya said to have revived worship here.
  • 2013: Major floods; temple survived though the town was heavily damaged.
  • Recent years: Rebuilt infrastructure and improved pilgrim facilities.

Why Devotees Come

To trek through scenic valleys, receive darshan at one of the highest Shiva shrines, and feel the stillness of the snow-clad mountains.

The limited open season from late April/May to November makes each visit feel especially precious.
