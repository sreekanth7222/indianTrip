Sacred Destinations

Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib)

The holiest Gurdwara of Sikhism, shining in the middle of the Amrit Sarovar, known for its gold-plated sanctum and spirit of equality.

  • Location: Amritsar, Punjab
  • Founded: 16th century by Guru Ram Das
  • Also known as: Sri Harmandir Sahib, Darbar Sahib
  • Famous for: Langar (community kitchen), kirtan, and serene sarovar

The Story & Significance

The Golden Temple was conceived as a place open to all, with entrances on four sides symbolizing acceptance from every direction. Its langar serves free meals to thousands daily, becoming a living example of seva and equality.

Historic Timeline

  • 1577: Amritsar city established by Guru Ram Das.
  • 1581–1604: Construction of Harmandir Sahib and installation of the Guru Granth Sahib.
  • 19th c.: Maharaja Ranjit Singh decorates the shrine with marble and gold.
  • Modern era: Continues as a global center of Sikh faith and service.

Why Devotees Come

To listen to continuous kirtan, sit by the shimmering sarovar, and partake in the langar, where everyone eats together irrespective of background.

The night view of the illuminated shrine reflecting in the water is especially cherished by visitors.
